Expected Performance in the Premier League

Floris Dobber

Course Project as part of DS4200 S22: Information Visualization, taught by Prof. Ab Mosca, Data Visualization @ Khoury, Northeastern University.

Abstract

By comparing expected goals to actual performance, users can see which soccer teams are deviating from their expected performance. This project aims to compile data related to the English Premier League and create visualizations to represent performance at the team and individual player level. These visualizations will be useful to bring attention to undervalued players, as well as to teams that are beating expectations. The information gained from the visualizations can be used by scouts, bettors or casual fans to gain a deeper insight into the performance of teams and players.

Visualization Explanation

The visualization tool's intended use is to discover insights into the performance of teams and players in the English Premier League. One way to do this is by looking at overall underlying performance and seeing which teams are performing well in these metrics. By then comparing looking at actual performance, it is possible to check whether this underlying performance is translating to results. This same process can be performed for players, which is a great way to find potential hidden gems.
